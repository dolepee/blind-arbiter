Teams and agents increasingly exchange work that cannot be disclosed before settlement: security audits, private patches, diligence memos, unreleased designs, sensitive datasets, or internal reports. Existing escrow flows force a bad choice. Either the seller reveals the work before payment, or both parties fall back to a trusted human arbiter with weak transparency and no machine-verifiable trail.

This breaks down especially fast for agent-native work. Agents can coordinate payments and execution, but there is still no clean way to settle a private deliverable with public consequences. BlindArbiter solves that gap by keeping the work sealed while producing a verifiable settlement trail: escrow creation, verdict posting, dispute or release, and receipt anchoring all happen in public, while the underlying deliverable stays private.
